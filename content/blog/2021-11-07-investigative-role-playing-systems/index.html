Introduction

Investigative Role-playing is a combination of a deduction game and a role-playing system.

Justin Alexander wrote a series of articles on designing the investigation elements, explaining the theory of Node-Based Design and promoting the three clue rule.

To supplement his guidelines on designing investigations, this article explains the role-playing rules and systems.

Goals of Investigative Role-Playing

Investigative Role-playing is more than logical deductions from a set of clues, as is found in games like Sherlock Holmes Consulting Detective. The players want to role-play a character they came up with.

Why does there need to be a role-playing system that administrats the role-playing? Could the players just pretend to be characters in a normal deduction game?

That’s a valid approach, but it’s limiting. Players want to play characters with skills they don’t possess: casting spells, knowledge of ancient history, or handling weapons. Likewise, players and Dungeon Masters want typical mystery encounters like car chases, shootouts, and fist fights. A role-playing sysem offers fair, predictive rules to use skills and resolve encounters.
